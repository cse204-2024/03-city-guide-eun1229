About Chicago

Chicago, Illinois is the third largest city in the United States with a population of nearly 3 million people as of 2024. Its location on the shore of Lake Michigan makes the city with a unique landscape that intertwines modern architecture with the natural beauty of its surroundings. Chicago's culture has contributed much to the visual arts, food, music, theater, and more. Its culture, beautiful architecture, food, and variety of activities makes it a prime spot for tourists to visit.

Activities

  • Architecture River Cruise

    River architecture cruises typically last 45-90 minutes, during which an expert on Chicago's architecture explains buildings as you pass by. Tickets are typically around $60, but prices vary based length and company (i.e. Wendella, Chicago Architecture Center).

  • Visit Millennium Park

    Millenium Park is located in the heart of downtown Chicago and is home to the famous Bean. The park contains a theater, pavillion, garden, and more. Visiting the park comes at no cost.

  • Skydeck Chicago

    The skydeck is an observation deck on the 103rd floor of Willis Tower. The skydeck is made completely of glass and offers a complete view of Chicago from above. General admission tickets are $32.
